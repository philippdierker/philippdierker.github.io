About

Philipp Dierker

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in Social Demography at the Max Planck Institute for Demographic Research in Rostock, Germany, and a Visiting Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Department of Sociology, Princeton University (April–July 2026).

My research sits at the intersection of family sociology, demography, and epidemiology. I study how partnership transitions — entering, dissolving, and re-forming partnerships — shape health and well-being across the life course. I am particularly interested in single mothers, parental separation, and the role of genetics in shaping social outcomes.
